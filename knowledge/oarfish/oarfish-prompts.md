# Oar Fish Starter Prompt Library

Working prompts to run against the Oar Fish workspace. Use as-is or as templates. Group A is weekly content. Group B is launches. Group C is critique and analytics. Group D is Shopify and ops.

## Group A: weekly content

### A1: Monday hero
> Generate the Monday hero post for [creature] from Vol. [n]. One caption, three to five lines. Open with a fact. End on a CTA. Include the standard hashtag stack. Reference at least one winner from launch hero patterns.

### A2: Tuesday educational carousel
> Build a Tuesday educational carousel: "[topic, e.g. Why we don't restock]". Six to eight slides. Slide one is the claim. Slides two to seven are evidence. Final slide is the CTA. Caption supports without repeating the carousel.

### A3: Wednesday meme
> Wednesday meme. The format is [meme template]. The Oar Fish punchline is about [topic, e.g. wallet pain on drop day]. One caption beat, no explanation. Hashtags from the calendar.

### A4: Thursday BTS
> Thursday BTS post for the [creature] drop. The visual is [description, e.g. marker drawing on the desk next to the printed hoodie]. Caption is process-honest, three to four lines. No motivational framing.

### A5: Friday story carousel
> Friday lore carousel for [creature]. Six to ten slides. Slide one hooks with a question or rumour. Slides two to seven build the mythology in Oar Fish voice. Final slide links to the drop. Caption stays out of the way.

### A6: Saturday social proof
> Saturday social proof post. The customer is [@handle], wearing [product]. Caption mirrors what they said in their original post, does not paraphrase. Tag and credit. Hashtag stack from the calendar.

### A7: Sunday meme
> Sunday meme. Outfit-flex anti-normal energy. The visual is [description]. End on a brand line ("Be weird. Be free. Be Oar Fish." or similar), not a CTA.

## Group B: launches and campaigns

### B1: Drop launch pack
> New drop: [creature], [product type], Vol. [n]. Generate the full launch pack:
> - Shopify product copy
> - Email subject and body (announcement)
> - SMS announcement
> - Three TikTok hooks
> - Two Meta ad variants (structurally different)
> - Friday story carousel outline (eight slides)
> - Hero image art direction (creature pose, palette, environment)
>
> All assets share voice. Pull from winners for structure.

### B2: Tease to launch sequence
> Generate the seven-day pre-launch sequence for [creature]: tease (-7), reveal (-3), countdown (-1), launch (0). One asset per day across IG, TikTok, X, and email. Tease shows cropped illustration only, no creature reveal.

### B3: Last call or sold out
> The [product] is at [stock level: 30% / under 10 units / sold out]. Write the appropriate stage post. Tone: matter-of-fact, weird-confident, no manufactured urgency. If sold out, close the chapter cleanly.

### B4: Lookbook drop
> Lookbook for Vol. [n]. Generate:
> - Editorial intro paragraph (60 to 80 words)
> - Per-look caption (15 words max each)
> - Closing line in Oar Fish voice

### B5: Collaboration brief
> Draft a brief for [collaborator: artist | photographer | musician | videographer]. Include: brand context (one paragraph), what we want (specific deliverables), what we don't want (forbidden moves), three Oar Fish-aligned references, timeline, and creative latitude.

## Group C: critique and analytics

### C1: Brand voice audit
> Critique the following [post / email / ad]:
> [paste content]
> Flag every forbidden move by name. Score it out of ten on voice, specificity, and brand fit. Rewrite if it scores below seven.

### C2: Weekly content review
> Last week's posts and engagement numbers below. Identify:
> - The top performer and why
> - The weakest performer and why
> - One thing to repeat next week
> - One thing to retire
>
> Do not invent causation.

### C3: Drop post-mortem
> Vol. [n] launch data:
> [paste Shopify, ad, and email metrics]
> Output: what we know happened, what we don't know but suspect, the single biggest lesson for Vol. [n+1].

### C4: Funnel diagnostic
> Top of funnel: [reach numbers]
> Mid-funnel: [click and visit numbers]
> Bottom: [add-to-cart and conversion numbers]
> Where is the leak? Propose one diagnostic test, not three theories.

## Group D: Shopify and operations

### D1: Product page rewrite
> Rewrite the Shopify product page for [product] following the brand bible's product language structure. Include: title, short description, full description, three bullet features, care instructions, drop status note.

### D2: Collection copy
> Collection copy for [collection name]. 60 to 80 words. Open with the creature theme. Close with the drop ethos.

### D3: Email flow build
> Build the [welcome | abandoned cart | post-purchase | win-back] flow. Specify number of emails, send delays, subject lines, body copy, and CTAs. Voice consistent with the brand bible.

### D4: Image prompt for Midjourney or Flux
> Generate an image prompt for [Midjourney / Flux] in Oar Fish's marker-and-ink style. Subject: [creature]. Mood: [dark / oceanic / forest / desert / sky]. Include style anchors (marker texture, ink wash, hand-drawn linework, Oar Fish purple), aspect ratio, and negative prompts (no logos, no clean vector, no AI-glossy).
