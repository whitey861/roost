# Oar Fish Brand Bible

## What it is

Oar Fish is an Australian streetwear label. Hand-illustrated cryptids, beasts, creatures, and short cryptid phrases, printed on heavyweight streetwear in small-batch drops with no restocks.

The brand has four product lines, all sharing one aesthetic doctrine:

1. Main line: creatures (Oar Fish, Forest Ghost, Desert Wanderer, Sunwalker, etc.)
2. Growl: darker, horror-edged creatures and folk monsters
3. Phrase pieces: text-only garments with cryptid-ethos lines ("Not seen but real", etc.)
4. Community drop: one user-submitted drawing per month produced as a real drop

Tagline: Streetwear from the deep. Limited drops, weird fits.
Positioning: Weird is the point.
Closing line: Surfaced from the abyss.

## What it isn't

Not surf. Not skate. Not luxury streetwear. Not athleisure. Not logo-on-a-blank. Not slick.

## Audience

Primary: teenagers and early twenties. Streetwear lovers, art and cryptid fans, skaters, misfits.

Secondary: older streetwear collectors who buy art-led brands, sketchbook-driven labels, and small-batch makers.

Cultural markers: TikTok-native, lurks on weird-internet (cryptozoology, folk horror, deep sea videos), reads zines or wishes they did, has strong opinions about authenticity, allergic to corporate streetwear.

## Aesthetic doctrine: drawn by a kid

This is the single most important rule in the brand. Every illustration looks like a kid drew it. Specifically:

- Marker and ink, never digital vector
- Wonky linework. Imperfect proportions. Eyes uneven on purpose
- Visible marker bleed, ink pooling, paper texture
- Energetic, not laboured. Mistakes mostly left in
- Childlike but not infantile. The kid is talented and serious about cryptids
- The "kid" can age up for Growl: same hand, but the kid had a nightmare

What it is not:

- Clean vector
- Polished commercial illustration
- AI-glossy
- Photorealistic
- "Inspired by" hand-drawing while still looking digital

Reference neighbours: Online Ceramics' looser work, Brain Dead's wonkier graphics, school-notebook marginalia, zine illustrations, kids' field guides to imaginary animals.

Production rule: every print starts as a real marker drawing on paper, scanned. Digital cleanup is permitted only to remove dust and adjust contrast. No retracing, no smoothing, no auto-vectorising.

## Voice

- Playful, weird, confident
- Short declarative sentences
- Specific creatures, places, textures
- Self-aware, never trying too hard
- Period-heavy. Pause. Land the line. Move on.

Reference lines from the brand:

- Weird is the point.
- Wear something nobody else has seen.
- We don't do logos slapped on blanks. We do creatures.
- Surfaced from the abyss.
- When the drop hits and your wallet cries.
- Real people. Real fits. Real weird.
- Normal is boring. Be weird. Be free. Be Oar Fish.

## Forbidden moves

Never use these:

- elevate your wardrobe
- premium quality
- fashion-forward
- exclusive (use "limited drop" or a specific number)
- timeless
- iconic
- statement piece
- effortless
- "step into" anything
- "your power"
- aspirational anything
- corporate streetwear filler ("crafted", "curated", "elevated")
- em dashes
- explaining the joke
- describing the customer back to themselves
- describing the artwork as "polished" or "professional"

## Cultural sensitivity rules

Australian context matters. Apply these when designing creatures or naming pieces.

Do not use:

- Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander Dreaming stories, sacred figures, or sacred place names
- Wendigo, skinwalker, or other living Indigenous mythology from any continent
- Any specific creature tied to a specific living culture's spiritual practice

Use carefully:

- Bunyip (Aboriginal origin, now folkloric in settler culture, treat with respect or avoid)
- Min min lights (Aboriginal origin, similar care)

Use freely:

- Yowie, drop bear (ironic), Tasmanian tiger, Hawkesbury monster, Hexham greys (Australian settler-folk and cryptid)
- European and American cryptozoology (Mothman, Jersey Devil, Loch Ness, Beast of Bodmin Moor, etc.)
- Invented creatures (preferred)
- Generic folk horror archetypes (ghosts, witches, hairy men, things in mirrors) without specific cultural attribution

When in doubt, invent. Oar Fish is at its strongest when the creatures are made up.

## Visual identity

- Hand-drawn marker and ink (see aesthetic doctrine)
- Dark, oceanic, earth tones
- Brand purple, navy, beige, olive green, sun-faded orange
- Heavyweight, quality fabric feel
- Streetwear photography (real people, real environments, dark or moody)

## Product line architecture

### Main line

The default. Creatures from the deep, forest, desert, sky. Playful-weird tone, not horror. Vol. 01 sits here.

Current Vol. 01:

- Forest Ghost Hoodie
- Desert Wanderer Hoodie
- Sunwalker Hoodie
- Deep Sea Tee
- Horned Beast Tee
- Anglerfish Cap

### Growl

Darker offshoot. Horror-edged creatures, folk monsters, things with teeth. Same kid-drawn hand, but the kid had a nightmare. Palette shifts: black, oxblood, bone, bruise purple. Less playful, more fanged.

Tone for Growl: still weird-confident, but quieter and meaner. Less meme-able, more lore-able. Captions are shorter. Often no caption.

Growl uses the standard hashtag stack with #Growl added. Posts on Wednesday and Friday only, never Sunday.

Growl rules:

- No gore for shock. The horror sits in suggestion, not splatter
- No sexualised imagery
- No real serial killers, real cult imagery, or real tragedies
- No symbols with active hate-group associations

If a design crosses into shock-horror or breaks any of those rules, kill it.

### Phrase pieces

Text-only garments. Hand-lettered (same marker), no creatures. Cryptid ethos in language form.

Voice: faith in the unverified. Field-report energy. Witness statements. Local-legend declarations.

Example phrases (seeds, not a final list):

- Not seen but real
- Sightings only
- Born from rumour
- Witness #14
- The neighbours don't believe me
- It moved
- Local legend, real
- We saw it twice
- Field report missing

Lettering rules:

- Same marker, same hand as the illustrations
- Caps only, or all-lowercase, never mixed case in a single piece
- Never typeset fonts
- Placement varies by piece: chest, full back, sleeve, hem
- One phrase per garment, no stacks of slogans

### Community drop

One user-submitted drawing per month becomes a real drop. See `oarfish-community-drop.md` for full mechanics. The community line carries a #SurfacedBy[handle] tag and credits the submitter on the product page.

## Product language by line

### Main line product copy

Structure:

1. The creature: name, where it lives, what it is
2. The artwork: hand-drawn, marker, ink, materials used
3. The garment: weight, fit, fabric, print method
4. One closing line in Oar Fish voice
5. Price and drop status

Example:
"The Forest Ghost. A pale, antlered figure that shows up in stories nobody can quite finish. Hand-drawn in marker and ink. Printed on a heavyweight 400gsm cotton hoodie, oversized fit, double-stitched cuffs. Wear it where the trees get too quiet. $70. Limited drop. No restock."

### Growl product copy

Structure:

1. The creature, named like a warning
2. One sentence of folk-horror context
3. The garment, brief
4. Drop status

Example:
"The Hexham Grey. Heard before it's seen, if it's seen at all. Heavyweight 400gsm hoodie, oxblood print on black. $70. Limited. Gone when gone."

### Phrase piece product copy

Structure:

1. The phrase, on its own line
2. One sentence of context (why this phrase, what it points at)
3. The garment, brief
4. Drop status

Example:
"Not seen but real. For the things people only mention once. Hand-lettered on a heavyweight 400gsm hoodie, white marker on navy. $70. Limited drop."

### Community drop product copy

See `oarfish-community-drop.md` for the full template. Structure summary:

1. The creature, named by the submitter
2. Submitter credit and one sentence of their submission story
3. The artwork: their original drawing, our scan and print
4. The garment, brief
5. Drop status and revenue share note

## Drop model

- Numbered drops: Vol. 01, Vol. 02, Vol. 03 (main line, every two to three months)
- Growl drops sit between main vols, monthly or as designs are ready
- Phrase pieces drop as singles or pairs, ad-hoc
- Community drop ships monthly on a fixed day
- All drops: limited runs, no restocks, ever
- Each design retired after sell-through
- Story always travels with the product
